Welcome to the Philippines

The Philippines is a beautiful, vibrant country with 7,600 islands to explore.

Filipinos are known for a rich culture that blends indigenous, Asian, and western influences. They are known for being warm and hospitable people with a high value for family and interpersonal relationships.

This is my favorite place I like to go and visit as well as spend my quality time with friends and families.


Traditional Dance

  • Tinikling - Known as the national dance of the Philippines, it involves dancers stepping over and between bamboo poles in a rhythmic pattern.
  • Cariñosa - A traditional folk dance characterized by its flirtatious movements and use of handkerchiefs.
  • Pandanggo sa Ilaw - A dance that involves balancing oil lamps on the head and hands while gracefully moving.
  • Maglalatik - A mock-war dance representing a fight over coconut meat, performed by male dancers with coconut shell halves on their bodies.
  • Itik-Itik - A dance that mimics the movements of ducks, with dancers imitating the gestures of wading and flying ducks.


Traditional Food

  • Adobo - A popular dish made with chicken or pork marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices.
  • Sinigang - A sour soup made with meat (often pork) and a variety of vegetables, flavored with tamarind.
  • Lechon - A whole roasted pig, often served during festive occasions and celebrations.
  • Pancit - Various noodle dishes, often stir-fried with vegetables, meat, and seafood.
  • Halo-Halo - A refreshing dessert made with shaved ice, milk, various fruits, sweet beans, and topped with ice cream.


Philippine Culture

  • Family Comes First - Family is highly important to Filipino Culture. Children are taught to say ‘po’ and ‘apo,’ showing respect to their grandparents, from an early age. Extended families usually live together and even distant members are given the title of cousin.
  • Filipinos are all about helping one another - Have you ever experienced the bayanihan in the Philippines? This comes from the Filipino word “bayan”, which means nation, town or community. In a nutshell, it’s when people join together to help out with real ‘community spirit’.

What the Philippines is Known For

  • Beaches and Islands - The Philippines is famous for its beautiful beaches and over 7,000 islands, including popular destinations like Boracay, Palawan, and Cebu.
  • Festivals - Vibrant festivals such as Sinulog, Ati-Atihan, and Pahiyas showcase the rich culture and traditions of the Filipino people.
  • Hospitality - Filipinos are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature.
  • Diverse Culture - A blend of various influences, including Malay, Spanish, American, and indigenous cultures.
  • Natural Wonders - Home to unique natural attractions like the Chocolate Hills, Mayon Volcano, and the Banaue Rice Terraces.
